About Our Calendar System

Welcome to our dual calendar system that bridges the Ethiopian and Gregorian calendars. This tool helps you easily convert and view dates between these two important calendar systems.

Ethiopian Calendar

The Ethiopian calendar is a solar calendar that originated in Egypt. It has 13 months, with 12 months of 30 days each and a final month of 5 or 6 days. It is approximately 7-8 years behind the Gregorian calendar.

Gregorian Calendar

The Gregorian calendar is the most widely used calendar today. It has 12 months of varying lengths (28-31 days) and was introduced by Pope Gregory XIII in 1582.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are the dates different between the two calendars?

The Ethiopian calendar follows a different solar cycle and started counting years differently. It is generally 7-8 years behind the Gregorian calendar.

What is the thirteenth month in the Ethiopian calendar?

The thirteenth month is called Pagume and has 5 or 6 days depending on whether it's a leap year.

How accurate is the conversion?

Our conversion system uses precise astronomical calculations and established conversion algorithms to ensure accuracy between both calendar systems.
